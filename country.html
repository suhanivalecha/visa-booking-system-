USA

    The United States offers various visa options for travelers based on their purpose of visit. The most common visa types include:
  1. Tourist (B-2) Visa: For individuals traveling to the US for tourism, vacation, or visiting family and friends.
  2. Business (B-1) Visa: For individuals traveling to the US for business purposes, such as meetings, conferences, or negotiations.
  3. Student (F-1) Visa: For individuals seeking to study at an accredited US institution.
  4. Work (H-1B) Visa: For skilled workers employed by US companies.

UK

The United Kingdom offers several visa options for visitors, depending on the purpose and duration of stay:
  1. Standard Visitor Visa: For tourism, visiting family or friends, or business-related visits.
  2. Tier 4 (General) Student Visa: For individuals aged 16 or over who wish to study in the UK.
  3. Tier 2 (General) Work Visa: For skilled workers sponsored by a UK employer.
  4. Tier 1 (Investor) Visa: For high-net-worth individuals making a significant financial investment in the UK.

JAPAN

Japan offers various visa options for visitors, including: Tourist Visa: For individuals visiting Japan for sightseeing, recreation, or visiting friends and relatives. Work Visa: For individuals intending to work in Japan, either as skilled professionals or under specific categories such as intra-company transferees or engineers. Student Visa: For individuals enrolled in a Japanese educational institution. Working Holiday Visa: Available for citizens of certain countries, allowing them to engage in short-term work and travel in Japan. Requirements for Japanese visas typically include a valid passport, visa application form, recent photographs, proof of financial means, and sometimes a Certificate of Eligibility issued by a sponsor in Japan.
